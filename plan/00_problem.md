
**The life problem**

Case 1:
Our parents are aging. In typical family dynamics, that falls on a single person who's willing to sacrifice their time and energy to help care for them; from scheduling doctors appts, paying bills, making sure they are ok, getting them to and from things. There exists plenty of apps and messaging platforms to coordinate this, but it's all over the place and isn't very collaborative. On top of that, the caretaker's sibling always wants to know what the doctor said but lives across the country and it's only making the caretaking harder; keeping them up to date is another job for someone already at their limit.

Case 2:
Taking care of the kids is a full time job. It often falls on one parent who carries the invisible load of managing their schedules. Making sure they are getting to and from school and after school activities. That they have everything they need for school, and have finished their work. Coordinate with Jan about picking up her kids or is she taking yours to soccer practice and when even is practice today?

Case 3:
We have a cute puppy but he's a lot. My partner and I need to coordinate who's feeding him and when. He has a medication now that needs to be taken twice a day and it's important to be at a certain time without overlaps. This is a very important deadline  and someone needs to verify it was done. Maybe if it's one person's job this time I just want to check if they did it or not without bugging them (avoid un-necessary stress.)

Case 4 (maybe):
I have a rehab plan for my lower back pain. The PT told me the stretches to do but I can't remember them or keep track of which days I was supposed to do these. Why do I have to look in multiple places just to keep track of this? What if I forgot how to do a certain stretch?

Case 5 (maybe):
I am a personal trainer and nutritionist who builds workout plans for many clients. I'd like a way to build these perscriptions and send them to clients who can manage their plan, tick off if they did each item of the plan. Or maybe they need a way to log their nutritional input.

**The business**
I was thinking there should be an app and website you can manage all these things. it should have features like
- Creating lists either manually or just conversationally with AI and have it extract TODOs for you
- Allowing seamless collaboration with other people on those todo lists
- Making it easy to have a conversation around todos or easy to modify plans
- The worst thing about todo apps is things change so this should take care of that
- It needs to think about who would have input on these plans or if say a parent being "cared for" has the ability to see these lists or even complete things on the list. In the case of a pet, they won't be contributing but a kid might if part of their plan is a chore list or something they take ownership of. Maybe there's some kind of controls around who can see what aspects of a plan
- Using conversational AI to make / edit / complete plans or todos is essential. For an older person, being able to open the app and push the conversation button and ask "what should i do today" and have it break down the steps. They do some of them, come back and tell the app and it just gives them easy to follow next steps.
- For case 2, there are a ton of apps for coordinating kid programs. They are all over the place (email, texts, band app, group me, mojo, etc). It would be amazing to just take all this input and condense it into single SPECIFIC to your kid steps. If they are on a baseball team called the Tigers but the coach emails 4 different teams with a blanket email, we should extract the things that actually relate and if there are deadlines we need to build that into concerns (photo day is saaturday 12pm at Olive park, make sure you have your hat and black pants.) (Cheer practice is 3-5pm today but it might end early if they finish the routine.)
- If there are 3 kids caring for a parent the system should identify ways to distribute the load, breaking up tasks (driving mom to apt can only be completed by someone nearby but following up with mom later that evening could be either kid who could record notes about it.) 
- The system could intelligently see someone is carrying more and prompt the other caretakers to step up, or even check in with the overloaded caretaker and give them praise/appreciation.
- for deadbeat kids (loving term) it could say "hey you haven't asked dad about his apt with dr Guy, want to message him?" and that could prefab a text message that is caring and has details about the apt.

**Confusing Points**
It could get really confusing with how many things are being managed, the app / website would need to have a strong sense of "what's actually next" and let you quickly take things off your mental load, kinda of like GTD methodology.

If caring for a parent with medical records, we either have to find a HEPA certified approach or need to make sure they aren't violating rules by storing something in a way that's not allowed.

Conversation around a care patient might be sensitive. Make it easy to understand who can see certain messages is crucial.

**Naming**
We need to brainstorm names for both the app, the caretakers, the patients, the plans, the concerns, etc. It should all be cohesive and safe/friendly/peaceful.
